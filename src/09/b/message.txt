Keio University carries on the legacy of its founder Yukichi Fukuzawa 
as it strives to be an institution where individuals think 
independently, take action, and set the course of their own the future. 
Since its founding in 1858, Keio has been guided by the principles 
of independence and self-respect. At Keio, we don't stop at 
simply acquiring academic knowledge, having long recognized 
the importance of applying the fruits of its scholarship to society. 
As a comprehensive educational institution encompassing 
all levels from elementary school to graduate school, 
Keio brings together students, faculty, staff, and alumni 
into a shared community of learning, where each member contributes 
to the creation of new value in their respective fields. As Keio 
continues to engage with society through education, research, 
and healthcare, it is also pursuing a variety of new initiatives, 
such as becoming an "AI-Native University," to cultivate 
the knowledge and skillsets that can meet the changing demands 
of the times.
